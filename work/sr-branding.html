Self-initiated Identity · 2018

SR_
Branding.

A self-initiated identity and visual language exploration — a sandbox for working out how a brand system can carry meaning without leaning on a logo.

Role
Designer · solo
Type
Self-initiated
Scope
Identity system
Year
2018
01Premise

A brand without a logo.

Most identity work treats the logo as the load-bearing element — every other decision exists to support it. This exploration started from the opposite premise: what if the logo were the least interesting thing, and the brand lived in everything else? Typography, grid, color, motion, voice.

02System

Marks as punctuation, not portraits.

The visual language treats the brand mark as a piece of typography — an underscore, a punctuation choice — rather than a self-contained image. That decision opens up the rest of the system to do real expressive work.
