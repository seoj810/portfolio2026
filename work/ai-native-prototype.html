AI-First Communication

Technology is shifting. We are moving away from just "apps" and toward "agents." UX must shift from navigating interfaces to declaring intent. In the world of communication, this means the technology doesn't just send the message - it understands what you are trying to get done.

The 4 Levels of AI Agents

There are four levels of agent participation.

Level 1: Direct Connection (Today)

The AI is just a voice-activated dialer.

Example: You say, "Call Mom on WhatsApp," and it immediately opens the line. It does not screen or help; it just connects.

Level 2: Background Assist

The AI stays in the background of your conversation to help.

Example: On a group call planning a trip, it listens, checks calendars, and shows best dates in real time.

Level 3: Negotiated Handoff

The AI handles the boring setup and handoff work.

Example: It calls the school, waits on hold, explains the issue, and pings you when the right teacher is on the line.

Level 4: Full Delegation

The AI handles the entire task from start to finish.

Example: "Get my flight refunded." It calls the airline, resolves it, and notifies you when the money is back.

Level 4

Full Delegation

Our focus is on Full Delegation.

Communication is really about relationships - talking to your mom, your friends, or your community.

We want to automate the logistics so you can focus on the people.

Personal Agent

Imagine a world where everyone had their own personal AI agent?

These agents could make small decisions and handle the heavy lifting of coordination.

Agent-to-Agent

In this future, your AI talks directly to your friend's AI.

Instead of a messy group chat, a web of digital assistants works behind the scenes.

They solve problems and schedule events instantly.

The Social Profile

For this to work, there must be trust.

Each user controls a digital contract (SOCIAL.md) that tells their AI what it can share, what stays private, and which tools other agents can access.

Clear rules make delegation safe.

Learnings / Constraints

Relationships are complex. To get this right, agents need a way to map the nuances of how people relate to one another.

Social etiquette, emotional cues, reading the room. These are hard problems. Are our agents intelligent enough to factor all of this in?

Safety and privacy are critical, and the key to making any of this work.

A social profile may not be a simple list. It may need to be far more complex to truly capture someone's social comfort.
